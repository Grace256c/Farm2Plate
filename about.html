✍️ How It Started

From a Conversation
Under a Mango Tree

In 2021, our co-founders — a food systems specialist, a Python engineer, a nutritionist, a logistics expert, and a finance leader — sat together in a small village in Luwero District, Uganda.

They watched a farmer discard a full sack of tomatoes because he had no buyer, no transport, and no way to reach households just 20 kilometres away who desperately needed food.

That moment crystallised everything. The problem wasn't a shortage of food. It was a broken connection between those who grow and those who eat.

"The food exists. The hunger exists. We just needed to build the bridge." — Tisha Patience, CEO & Founder

Farm 2 Plate Connect was built to be that bridge — using SMS technology, AI, and mobile money to create a system that works for every Ugandan, even those without smartphones or internet access.

🧠 How We Think

Our Approach to Lasting Change

We don't just deliver aid. We rebuild systems so communities can feed themselves — permanently.

01

Root Cause Focus

Every feature we build targets a structural cause of food insecurity — broken markets, missing transport links, information gaps — not just symptoms.

02

Inclusive by Design

SMS-first technology means our platform reaches the last mile — farmers in remote villages with no data coverage, using phones worth UGX 15,000.

03

Evidence-Driven

Every decision is grounded in Uganda-specific data, community feedback, nutritional science, and rigorous impact measurement frameworks.

04

Self-Sustaining Model

Platform fees, microfinance margins, and strategic partnerships fund operations — reducing dependence on donor cycles and ensuring long-term viability.

05

Radical Transparency

Every donation is tracked and reported. Farmers see real prices. Donors see verified impact. Nothing hidden, nothing fabricated.

06

African-Centred

Built in Uganda, by Ugandans, for Ugandans — in three local languages, respecting local food cultures, trade patterns, and community structures.
